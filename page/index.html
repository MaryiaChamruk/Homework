ANTARCTIC

ANTARCTIC: THE LARGEST DESERT IN THE WORLD Location: Antarctica. Area: 13.8 million km2. Average temperature: in winter about -65 °C, in summer about -40 °C. The largest desert in terms of area is the Antarctic. Its area is 13.8 million km2 - it's like uniting the territory of Canada and India. It is also the most beautiful lifeless place on Earth. And cold. It was here that a record low temperature of -98 ° C was recorded in 2018. Only a few can survive in a desert characterized by a harsh climate. Therefore, the largest desert in the world is characterized by poor flora and fauna. The vegetation is represented by lichens, mosses and algae. Some insects, birds live among the animals, and whales, sperm whales, killer whales and pinnipeds live in the waters.


SAHARA

SAHARA: AFRICA'S LARGEST DESERT Location: Algeria, Egypt, Libya, Mauritania, Mali, Morocco, Niger, Sudan, Tunisia, Chad and Western Sahara. Area: 9.4 million km2. Average temperature: in winter about +13 °C, in summer about +37 °C. The largest desert on the mainland can be compared in area with the United States, and it continues to grow. According to forecasts, Lake Chad will dry up by 2040, and nothing will hold back the advance of the Sahara. It wasn't always like that. In ancient times, the Sahara desert was a region with a moderately humid climate. Changes in the Earth's orbit and the shrinking of the polar ice sheets caused alternating periods of rain and drought, and gradually the region became what we know it to be. (Climate change continues. Snow has fallen in the Sahara for the fifth time in the past 42 years.) The flora and fauna of the Sahara represent an amazing world that is not as monotonous as it seems to us. True, human settlements are formed only in oases, and 2.5 million people live in the desert, which is about five times less than in Moscow.
